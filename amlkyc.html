AML/KYC Policy

This page outlines the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) measures implemented on the Kittrow platform

Why AML and KYC Matter

The Foundation of Trust

AML and KYC policies are not just bureaucratic requirements — they are the foundation of trust between a platform and its users.

By verifying users' identities and monitoring transactions, Kittrow prevents the platform from being used for illicit activities such as money laundering, terrorism financing, tax evasion, or fraud.

KYC Procedure at Kittrow

Identity Verification Steps

Each user is required to undergo a simple verification process before making exchanges. This helps us confirm that every transaction is performed by a real, verified individual.

Required Documents

To complete KYC, you may be asked to provide:

A valid government-issued photo ID (e.g. passport)

Proof of address (not older than 3 months)

In some cases, we may request a selfie with your ID to ensure authenticity.

AML Monitoring and Controls

Transaction Oversight

Kittrow has a built-in system that continuously monitors transactions for unusual behavior. If suspicious activity is detected, the system may flag the transaction for manual review.

Compliance and Reporting

We comply with local and international regulations and report suspicious transactions to the relevant authorities when required by law.

Timing and Security

Average Verification Time

In most cases, verification is completed within 5 to 30 minutes. If your case requires manual review, it may take up to 24 hours.

Data Security

All your information is encrypted and stored securely. Access is granted only to authorized personnel, and we never share your data with third parties unless legally obligated.

Summary: What You Need

To start exchanging on Kittrow, you only need a few essentials:

1. A valid email address

2. A crypto wallet

3. A passport or national ID

We’ve designed the process to be quick, clear, and non-technical.
